Stay Safe Online Checklist for College Students in India

Secure Your UPI/Financial Apps: Never share your UPI PIN with anyone, including bank officials, and
avoid clicking on unknown collect request links sent via SMS or messaging apps.


Enable Two-Factor Authentication (2FA): Activate 2FA on all financial apps and social media accounts to 
add an extra layer of security beyond just a password.


Verify UPI IDs Carefully: Before sending money, double-check the recipients name and UPI ID to ensure 
you are transferring funds to the correct person.


Use Strong, Unique Passwords: Create complex passwords using a mix of letters, numbers, and symbols for 
each online service, and use a password manager to keep track of them.


Be Wary of Phishing Links: Do not click on suspicious links in emails, text messages, or DMs offering freebies,
scholarships, or jobs, as they often lead to phishing websites.


Keep Software Updated: Regularly update your phones operating system, apps, and antivirus software to patch 
security vulnerabilities.


Protect Personal Information: Avoid sharing sensitive details like your Aadhaar number, bank account details, 
or date of birth on public platforms or with unauthorized individuals.


Use Secure Public Wi-Fi: Avoid accessing financial apps or logging into important accounts while connected to public 
Wi-Fi networks, or use a Virtual Private Network (VPN) for security.


Reporting Channel: If you experience any online fraud or security breach, report it immediately to the National Cyber Crime Portal
(cybercrime.gov.in) or call the helpline number 1930.
